Small rooms

I've been thinking about how the best conversations I've had this year happened in small rooms — literally and otherwise. A kitchen table, not a conference hall. A group chat of four, not a Discord server of four thousand.

There's something about scale that changes the nature of a space. In a small room, you can say something half-formed and someone will help you finish the thought. In a large one, you say the polished version or you say nothing.

Building smaller, on purpose

I think this is part of why I write here instead of somewhere with more reach. A small room, even online, lets the writing stay half-formed sometimes. It doesn't have to perform. It just has to be true.

I don't think bigger rooms are wrong. Some ideas need the reach. But I'm noticing I want to build more small rooms this year, not fewer — the kind where the walls are close enough that you can hear each other think.
