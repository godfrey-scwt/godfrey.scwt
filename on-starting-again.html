On starting again

There's a particular kind of quiet that comes before starting something again after a long pause. Not the excitement of the first time — that's gone, spent on the version of you that didn't know yet how hard it would be. This is a quieter kind of willingness. Less romantic. More durable, maybe.

I stopped writing here for almost eight months. Not dramatically — no single day where I decided to quit. Just a slow drift, the kind where each week makes the next one easier to skip, until skipping becomes the default and writing becomes the exception.

What brought me back wasn't inspiration. It was noticing that I'd started narrating my days in my head again — turning a walk, a conversation, a small frustration into sentences before I'd even decided to write them down. That's usually the sign. The writing starts before I sit down to do it.

The myth of momentum

I used to think consistency was the whole game — that if I could just not break the chain, everything else would follow. But chains break. Life doesn't care about your streak. What matters more, I think, is how quickly you're willing to start again after it breaks, and how little shame you attach to the gap.

The gap isn't the failure. Treating the gap as a reason not to return is.

So here I am, starting again, with no promises about frequency. Just the intention to keep noticing things worth writing down, and the willingness to actually do it when I notice them.
