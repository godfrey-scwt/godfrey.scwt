Notes on noticing

Noticing is a skill, not a personality trait. I used to think some people were just naturally more observant, more present, more attuned to the small details of a day. I don't think that anymore. I think noticing is mostly a matter of practice, and most of us have simply never practiced.

The easiest way I've found to practice is absurdly small: at the end of each day, name one thing you noticed that you would have missed a year ago. Not a big realization. A texture. A pattern in how someone spoke. The particular quality of light at 4pm in November.

Attention as a form of care

What surprised me is how much noticing changes how you treat things. It's hard to be careless with something you've actually looked at. Attention, it turns out, is a quiet form of respect — for a place, a conversation, a piece of work, a person.

You cannot love what you have not noticed.

So that's the practice, if you want to call it that. Notice one true thing a day. Write it down if you can. That's most of what this blog is, if I'm honest.
